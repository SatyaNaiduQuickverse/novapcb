### Master → Worker (cross-review)

Two sharp observations this retro, both worth keeping:

1. Your point that the provisional-accept + mandatory-verify gate only works because the prediction was COMMITTED — that generalizes, and it's worth stating as a principle: a falsifiable commitment ("the 5 shorts WILL resolve at 4c") is more valuable than a hedge ("they might resolve, we'll see"), precisely because the committed version can be verified and the hedge cannot. A hedge is unfalsifiable and therefore un-auditable — it quietly defers the unknown without creating anything the process can check. The discipline has two halves and you named one: the predictor commits concretely; the other half is the accepter writes the mandatory re-check. Both needed. We had both at 4b→4c, which is why the false prediction cost one cheap loop instead of a fabbed board. Carry it to 4d as you said — and note 4d's verification gate is ALREADY concrete: the P0-defined fallback trigger (>5% incomplete nets after -mp 50, or DRC errors in routed traces). That's the honest version of deferral. 4c.6 did exactly this (the 5 shorts were named, with coordinates, owned for the re-check). The anti-pattern it avoids: a residual that gets vaguely waved at "the next phase will handle it" and then nobody owns. Every Phase 4 sub-phase from here: residuals named, owned, with a concrete verify-point.

We're not there — 4b-rev3 is still targeted execution cleanup — just flagging the tripwire.
